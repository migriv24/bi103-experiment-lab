# Experimental Design Lab — Asset Checklist

**Experiment:** Does adding two lower-body strength sessions per week make my easy runs faster?
**Outcome slot:** Science Skills (portfolio `science-skills`). Accent: science blue. Theme: blueprint / lab protocol.
**Grounding paper:** Ronnestad, B. R., & Mujika, I. (2014). *Optimizing strength training for running and cycling endurance performance: A review.* Scandinavian Journal of Medicine & Science in Sports, 24(4), 603-612. (Finding: strength training improves running economy.)

## How to draw / send
- Same as your other labs: dark pencil on white paper, one subject per page, photographed flat.
- White background is fine (no transparency needed). They get tinted/multiplied onto the blue blueprint.
- Save into `images/` using the exact filenames below so the build can find them.

## Drawings needed

### Core (the experiment's moving parts) — draw these first
- [ ] `dumbbell.jpeg` — a dumbbell or kettlebell (the independent variable: strength training)
- [ ] `watch.jpeg` — a stopwatch or your Galaxy watch (the dependent variable: pace / time)
- [ ] `leg_muscles.jpeg` — a leg with the main muscles, lightly labeled (quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves). *Reuse option: a leg/muscle drawing from the Movement lab.*
- [ ] `runner.jpeg` — a small running figure in motion (the activity). *Reuse option: the body photo from Soft Running.*
- [ ] `lightbulb.jpeg` — a lightbulb (the hypothesis)
- [ ] `calendar.jpeg` — a weekly grid, ~6 weeks, with run days and lift days marked (the protocol timeline)

### Supporting (controls + measurement)
- [ ] `sun.jpeg` — a sun / sunrise (controlled variable: same morning time)
- [ ] `route.jpeg` — a looping route or a treadmill (controlled variable: same course)
- [ ] `heart.jpeg` — a heart (resting heart-rate measurement). *Reuse option: a heart you already drew.*
- [ ] `notebook.jpeg` — a clipboard or lab notebook (recording the data)
- [ ] `paper.jpeg` — a journal article / research paper (the cited study)

### Optional flourishes (nice but skippable)
- [ ] `arrow_up.jpeg` — an up arrow (improvement / predicted result)
- [ ] `question.jpeg` — a question mark (the research question)
- [ ] `magnifier.jpeg` — a magnifying glass (analysis)

## Reuse, don't redraw

## Page sections the assets map to
1. Research question (question / lightbulb)

3. Hypothesis (lightbulb)
